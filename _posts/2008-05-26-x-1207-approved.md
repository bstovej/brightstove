<div id="msgcns!9091E2A0F1B7D523!681" class="bvMsg"><div>X.1207 &quot;Guidelines for Telecommunication Service Providers and End-users for Addressing the Risk of Spyware and Potentially Unwanted Software&quot; was determined and undergone a six months review by ITU-T members from Oct 2007 to April 2008 (see <a href="http://eussion.spaces.live.com/default.aspx?_c01_BlogPart=blogentry&amp;_c=BlogPart&amp;handle=cns!9091E2A0F1B7D523!551" target="_blank">my earlier blog entry on this topic</a>). In the April 2008 ITU-T SG 17 meeting in Geneva, the draft Recommendation was approved. It is now a formal ITU-T X.1207 Recommendation. Softcopy (in different formats) of the Recommendation is now available from ITU-T web site at <a href="http://www.itu.int/rec/T-REC-X.1207-200804-P/en">http://www.itu.int/rec/T-REC-X.1207-200804-P/en</a>. </div>
<div> </div>
<div>Besides X.1207, ITU-T SG 17 has also published a number of other cybersecurity related standards following the April 2008 meeting. Please read details at <a href="http://www.itu.int/ITU-T/newslog/CategoryView,category,Study Group 17.aspx" target="_blank">ITU-T Study Group 17 Web log entry</a>.</div>
